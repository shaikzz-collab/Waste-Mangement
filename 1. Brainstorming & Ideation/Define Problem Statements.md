# Defining Problem Statements

This document defines the core ecological and operational problems that **WasteGuide AI** is designed to address.

---

## 1. Context & Global Waste Crisis

Over **2.01 billion tons** of municipal solid waste are generated globally every year, with at least 33% of it not managed in an environmentally safe manner. High-income countries produce disproportionately large volumes of dry recyclables, yet their recycling rates hover between only **15% to 35%**. 

The fundamental cause of low recycling rates is not the lack of recycling plants, but rather **consumer sorting errors at the point of disposal**.

---

## 2. Problem Statements Identified

### Problem Statement 1: Contamination in Recycling Streams
- **Detail**: When non-recyclable materials (e.g. food-soiled cardboard, greasy pizza boxes, Styrofoam) are placed in recycling bins, they contaminate entire batches of clean materials. Contaminated batches cannot be processed and are redirected to landfills, increasing waste volumes.
- **Impact**: Increased sorting costs for cities, reduced value of scrap materials, and lost resource recovery opportunities.

### Problem Statement 2: The "Wishcycling" Phenomenon
- **Detail**: Well-intentioned citizens often throw items into recycling bins hoping they are recyclable, without checking municipal guidelines (e.g., throwing plastic bags or LED bulbs in curbside bins). Plastic bags catch in automated sorting machinery, causing sorting shutdowns.
- **Impact**: Maintenance shutdowns at Material Recovery Facilities (MRFs) costing thousands of dollars per hour, and increased safety risks for facility workers.

### Problem Statement 3: Public Confusion Over Complex Rules
- **Detail**: Recycling guidelines vary by city, county, and region. A plastic type accepted in San Francisco may be rejected in Oakland. Traditional static websites or paper flyers fail to provide immediate, context-specific guidance when a resident is standing in front of a bin.
- **Impact**: Cognitive fatigue leading to residents giving up on sorting entirely and throwing all items into general waste.

---

## 3. Targeted Solution: WasteGuide AI

To solve these problems, **WasteGuide AI** acts as a real-time, zero-friction educational interface:
- **Instant AI Categorization**: Users scan/type any item name and get a clear, local-first sorting decision.
- **Interactive Facilities Locator**: Connects users to correct specialized drop-offs for hazardous and electronic waste (e.g., LED bulbs, batteries), preventing toxic chemicals from reaching landfills.
- **Empirical Dashboard**: Reinforces correct recycling behaviors by showing personal statistics and positive environmental metrics.
